Credit for Prior Learning

Already certified as an EMT or AEMT? You may be eligible to receive college credit for your existing certifications and experience.

What is Credit for Prior Learning?

Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) recognizes that learning happens in many settings. If you hold a national or state certification in Emergency Medical Services, you may be eligible to receive college credit for courses that align with your existing knowledge and skills. This helps you progress through the program more efficiently.

EMT Certification Holders

Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)

National Registry or Virginia State Certification

If you possess a national or state certification in Emergency Medical Technician (EMT), you may be eligible to receive credit for the following courses:

  • HLT 105 Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation 1 Credit
  • EMS 111 Emergency Medical Technician 7 Credits
  • EMS 120 Emergency Medical Technician - Clinical 1 Credit

Total Potential Credits: 9

AEMT Certification Holders

Advanced Emergency Medical Technician (AEMT)

National Registry or Virginia State Certification

If you possess an Advanced Emergency Medical Technician (AEMT) certification, you may be eligible to receive credit for the EMT courses listed above, plus the following additional courses:

  • EMS 123 EMS Clinical Preparation 1 Credit
  • EMS 170 ALS Internship 1 Credit
  • EMS 180 Advanced EMS Foundation 1 Credit
  • EMS 181 Advanced Airway & Shock Management 1 Credit
  • EMS 182 Advanced Airway & Shock Management Lab 1 Credit
  • EMS 183 Advanced Medical Care 2 Credits
  • EMS 184 Advanced Medical Care Lab 1 Credit
  • EMS 185 Advanced Trauma Care 2 Credits
  • EMS 186 Advanced Trauma Care Lab 1 Credit

Total Potential Credits (including EMT): 20

How to Apply for CPL

1
Gather Documentation

Collect copies of your current certifications (NREMT card, state license).

2
Schedule Appointment

Meet with an EMS Program advisor to discuss your eligibility.

3
Submit Application

Complete the CPL application and provide required documentation.

4
Receive Credit

Approved credits will be added to your transcript.
